Three doors, three price tags: which one can a mainland-China family actually open?

A fact-checked comparison of the three programs promoted in the WeChat articles you collected, evaluated specifically for residents of developed mainland cities (Shanghai, Beijing, Shenzhen, Guangzhou, Hangzhou, Suzhou, Nanjing). "Suitable" is judged on what each identity delivers; "achievable" is judged on capital thresholds, China's foreign-exchange rules, and PRC nationality law — the constraints the marketing articles mostly leave out.

Greece — residence permit (永居卡) Hong Kong — resident visa → PR (香港身份) Turkey — full citizenship (护照)
01

The verdict in one screen

The three products are not interchangeable: one is a low-cost EU residence card, one is a high-bar quasi-domestic status, one is a second passport. Suitability depends on the goal; achievability depends on how much of your wealth already sits legally outside the mainland.

GREECE · 希腊黄金签证

Cheapest ticket to Europe — but the famous €250k price is now the exception, not the rule

€400k–€800k typical ≈ ¥310万–¥630万 [4][5]

Renewable-forever residence permit tied to property ownership, no stay requirement, Schengen 90/180 mobility, 12 years of free public schooling or cheap international schools.[1] No change of nationality, so no conflict with your hukou or PRC passport.

Best fitMiddle-class HNW families (~¥300万–¥700万 deployable offshore) prioritizing children's education and a low-risk "Plan B".
HONG KONG · 新资本投资者入境计划 (New CIES)

The most compatible identity for mainlanders — and by far the highest bar

HK$30M net assets + HK$30M invested ≈ ¥2,700万+ [6][8]

No education / language / residence requirements; renew on a 2+3+3 track purely by maintaining the investment; PR after 7 years or unconditional stay, after which capital is freed.[2][6] Mainland applicants must first obtain a foreign permanent residency (e.g., a small-country green card).[10]

Best fitGenuinely high-net-worth families (¥3,000万+ verifiable) wanting HK education, low tax and dual-base living without giving up mainland ties.
TURKEY · 土耳其投资入籍

A recyclable $400k for a real second passport — with the biggest legal caveat for PRC citizens

US$400k property, sellable after 3 yrs ≈ ¥285万 [11][12]

Full citizenship in 6–12 months, family included, no residence requirement, and eligibility (not a guarantee) for the US E-2 treaty-investor route.[3][12] But PRC law does not recognize dual nationality — voluntarily naturalizing abroad can mean automatic loss of Chinese nationality if it comes to light.[14]

Best fitUS-oriented investors/entrepreneurs whose capital is already offshore and who accept the dual-nationality risk knowingly.

04

The mainland reality check — what the articles don't say

For a resident of Shanghai or Shenzhen, "achievable" is decided less by the program's rules than by three PRC-side constraints. This is where most plans quietly fail.

1 · Getting the money out is the real threshold

China's personal FX quota is US$50,000 per person per year, and the purchase application form explicitly states the quota may not be used for overseas property purchase, securities investment, or investment-linked insurance — these are unopened capital-account items.[15][16] So a €400k Greek flat or a $400k Istanbul flat cannot be legally funded by simply wiring salary savings abroad. Pooling relatives' quotas ("蚂蚁搬家" smurfing) is expressly prohibited and has produced criminal convictions for illegal FX purchase when amounts are large.[16]

  • Legal routes that do exist: wealth already offshore (overseas earnings, HK/US brokerage gains, offshore company dividends); QDII-type channeled products; or the one-time emigrant property transfer (个人财产对外转移) — which is only available after you have already obtained foreign PR, so it can fund life abroad but not the qualifying investment itself in most cases.[16]
  • Practical implication: all three programs are realistically open only to families who already hold sufficient offshore assets. This is precisely why the Turkey article's protagonists are "美股搭子" — their capital is already sitting in US brokerage accounts.[3]

2 · Nationality law splits the three options into two legal categories

Greece and Hong Kong grant residence; Turkey grants citizenship. Under the PRC Nationality Law (Art. 3: dual nationality not recognized; Art. 9: a Chinese national who settles abroad and voluntarily acquires a foreign nationality automatically loses Chinese nationality), a Turkish passport quietly held alongside a PRC passport and hukou is a legally unstable arrangement.[14] Consequences if surfaced (e.g., border data matching, CRS reporting, or renewing documents) can include cancellation of hukou and PRC documents. The Turkey article frames the passport as pure upside; for a mainland civil-servant-adjacent or SOE employee — including someone at a state-owned group like yours — this risk category deserves the most weight, and many employers' compliance rules add a second layer of restriction on foreign residency/nationality.

3 · Hong Kong's "detour" requirement adds a hidden step and hidden cost

The HK Immigration Department limits New CIES eligibility to foreign nationals, Macao/Taiwan residents, and Chinese citizens who have already obtained foreign permanent-resident status.[6] Mainland applicants therefore first buy a small-country PR (agents commonly cite Vanuatu or Guinea-Bissau), adding roughly US$15k–$50k and a compliance question mark that law firms advise vetting carefully.[10] Net-asset review (6 months of holdings, audited by an HK CPA) and source-of-funds scrutiny are real gates — crypto has been accepted as proof of assets in reported cases, but with full transaction and tax documentation.[18]

Compliance reminderThis brief is general research, not legal, tax, or investment advice. FX, nationality, tax-residency (CRS) and employer-specific rules interact case-by-case; engage a licensed immigration lawyer and a cross-border tax advisor before moving money or filing anything.
05

Program deep dives

Greece · Golden Visa 房产+永居

The low door has been raised — plan around €400k, not €250k

Status: EU + Schengen member · Permit: investor residence, family included (three generations) · Stay requirement: none · Citizenship path: ~7 yrs actual residence + Greek language test
What still holds from the article
  • Residence continues as long as the property is held; family (spouse, children, parents) included.[1]
  • Schengen mobility: 90 days per 180 across 29 states without extra visas.[1]
  • Education play is real: free public schooling, comparatively cheap international schools, plus a possible 华侨生联考 route back into top mainland universities once residence-abroad conditions are satisfied.[1]
What changed / what to watch
  • Since 31 Aug 2024 the tiers are €800k (Attica, Thessaloniki, Mykonos, Santorini, islands >3,100 pop.) and €400k elsewhere; €250k survives only for commercial-to-residential conversions and listed-building restorations — a narrower, execution-risk-heavy niche.[4][5]
  • €800k-zone properties must generally be single properties ≥120㎡; short-term-letting restrictions apply to some categories.[5]
  • The article's price-boom framing (+86% since 2017, Athens south +14.7%/yr) is the seller's pitch — entering after a 8-year run-up means buying high; golden-visa-earmarked stock often carries a premium.[1]
Hong Kong · New Capital Investment Entrant Scheme 新资本投资者入境计划

Not really "immigration" — a capital-for-status swap inside China's own system

Threshold: HK$30M net assets (6-month look-back) + HK$30M invested · Structure: ≥HK$27M permitted assets (stocks, bonds, funds; non-residential property capped at HK$15M; one residence priced ≥HK$30M capped at HK$10M) + HK$3M CIES portfolio (7-yr lock) · Visa: 2+3+3 · PR at year 7 or unconditional stay
Why it suits developed-city mainlanders best
  • Same country, same time zone, one-hour access from Shenzhen/Guangzhou; keeping mainland hukou is allowed even after converting to HK PR.[2]
  • No residence, work, education or language requirement to renew — the only real obligation is keeping HK$30M invested; it is the only mainstream HK route offering unconditional stay if PR residence tests aren't met.[2][6]
  • Momentum is on the applicant's side: asset-review window cut from 2 years to 6 months (Mar 2025), family co-owned assets countable, holding-company investment allowed (further eased Mar 2026), property rules relaxed (Sept 2025).[7][9] Applications passed 2,200 by late 2025 with ~HK$70bn expected inflow.[8]
Frictions the article downplays
  • The third-country-PR prerequisite for mainland Chinese citizens is structural, not a loophole — budget time and vetting for it.[6][10]
  • HK$30M sits in market assets for ~7 years: drawdowns are your problem; the HK$3M CIES portfolio is locked for 7 years regardless.[9]
  • Converting to PR still requires "ordinary residence" evidence in practice (home, schooling, tax footprint); pure absentee investors usually land on unconditional stay instead — fine for most purposes, but not a HKSAR passport.[6]
Turkey · Citizenship by Investment 投资入籍

The cleverest financial structure — and the heaviest legal asterisk

Threshold: US$400k real estate (bank-transfer only, SPK-licensed valuation, EIDS-verified) · Hold: 3 years (title-deed annotation) · Timeline: ~6–12 months · Family: spouse + children under 18 · Dual citizenship: allowed by Turkey — not by China
The genuine logic of the "美股搭子" pitch
  • Capital is parked, not burned: unlike Caribbean donation programs, the $400k returns when the property is sold after year 3, while citizenship is permanent.[3][11][17]
  • E-2 treaty eligibility is real and is the passport's core differentiator for US-oriented people: no lottery, no quota, renewable indefinitely, spouse can work, children attend US public schools.[3][12]
  • No residence requirement, fast processing, and (since 2026) same-day biometrics on a short visit.[17][11]
Where the article oversells
  • E-2 is not a ticket that comes with the passport. It additionally requires investing "substantial" capital into a real, operating US business you direct — typically another US$100k+ and an approvable business plan; it's a non-immigrant visa with no green-card path of its own.[12]
  • Visa-free count is ~110–120 (Henley 2026 rank ≈51st per one tracker), not obviously "global No.39"; and Schengen/UK/Canada/US all still require visas.[12][13]
  • Currency risk: 2025 Istanbul-area prices rose ~18% nominal but ~−10% real after inflation; a USD-denominated "money-back" plan depends on the exit market.[13]
  • And the decisive one for mainlanders: PRC non-recognition of dual nationality (see Section 04).[14]

So which is "more suitable and achievable"?

Ranking depends on the household's offshore liquidity and objective. For residents of developed mainland cities, the honest matrix looks like this:

Household profile (typical in SH/BJ/SZ/GZ/HZ/SZ/NJ)Recommended doorWhy
Upper-middle class: ¥300万–¥700万 deployable offshore; goal = kids' education + Plan B; wants zero nationality risk Greece (€400k zone) Lowest legal-risk product (residence only), lowest workable price, no stay requirement, education path both westward and back via 华侨生联考. Achievability hinges entirely on having the funds legally offshore.
Genuine HNW: ¥3,000万+ verifiable, business or investment income, wants dual-base Greater-Bay life, HK schooling, low tax Hong Kong New CIES Most compatible with keeping a mainland career, hukou and family structure; strongest education dividend (DSE / 华侨生联考); policy actively loosening. Highest bar, plus the third-country-PR step.
US-oriented trader / founder with US$500k+ already in offshore/US accounts; comfortable with legal gray zones; may eventually relocate to the US Turkey CBI Only option that returns the capital and yields a lifetime status; unique E-2 bridge to the US. Must consciously accept PRC dual-nationality exposure and prepare a real E-2 business, not just the passport.
Employees of SOEs / civil service / sensitive sectors (incl. state-owned group staff) None yet — Greece only, later, if at all Foreign nationality (Turkey) is typically incompatible with such roles; even foreign PR often triggers reporting duties. If planning at all, a pure residence product acquired after leaving such employment carries the least career risk.
Family with mainly onshore RMB wealth and no offshore base Realistically: none, for now The FX rules in Section 04 make every option non-compliant to fund from scratch. First-order task is building legal offshore income/assets, not choosing a program.

Bottom line: by cost, Greece is the most achievable; by fit with a mainland life, Hong Kong is the most suitable but is a genuinely rich-family product; Turkey is the most financially elegant but the least legally comfortable for a PRC national. The single biggest gate for all three is not the program — it is whether your capital is already, legally, outside the mainland.
